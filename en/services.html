LinkedIn mentoring

I have been working in LinkedIn for over 1.5 years. This is not “SMM for posts.” It is mentoring and ongoing support where we build the habit, find a comfortable line of content, and grow a personal brand with a system—even when it feels like there is nothing to write. We open with 1–2 one-hour interviews about your personal brand. Then—regular calls (often weekly), Telegram between calls, themes, networking, and metrics. If posting feels awkward or you want to vanish right after you publish—we treat it like any other block: smaller step, simpler angle.

I help you find a comfortable posting rhythm that creates new networking, and I keep you from drifting off the path—reminders, chat check-ins, honest return to what we agreed. We start from different baselines; content, cadence, networking, and personal brand still stay on one track.

What the retainer usually covers

Calls

First, 1–2 hour-long interviews about you and your brand. Then a steady rhythm (often weekly): progress, priorities, edits, and an honest look at what blocks you from posting.

Profile

Headline, About, experience, visuals—so a reader knows who you help and what to do next.

Content and themes

From “nothing to say” into structure: columns, formats, a plan, drafts. The aim is a habit you can sustain—one that feeds networking, not one-off spikes.

Networking

Connections, thoughtful comments, and DMs without pushy sales energy.

Metrics

We check what lands—reach, profile visits, conversations—and adjust focus.

Support and staying on the rails

When it feels scary or you want to quit, we handle it inside the retainer and pull you back to the rhythm: a steady presence and honest accountability between calls.

How the work flows

This is distilled from real check-ins and structured plans I use with clients. On the site it stays generic: no niche-specific promises, and no fixed counts for posts or connections until we align on your case. At its core the offer is system + habit—the engine behind content and networking—so you do not drop off halfway.

Opening interviews: 1–2 ~one-hour sessions

Questions, listening, facts and meaning on paper. The point is not to “fill a form,” but to help you see where you are already valuable and what can move into public view with care. That becomes the base for headline, About, and your content line—and it lowers the resistance of “I have nothing to say.”

Kickoff: goals and guardrails

After the interviews and admin, we align on your LinkedIn goal, realistic time, and a baseline: what the profile and activity look like today.

  • Goal: career, personal brand, inbound, networking—often mixed; we still pick a priority.
  • Baseline: simple markers that fit your situation.
  • Between calls: facts for the profile, a photo, a rough list of themes and columns—so we are not guessing in a vacuum.

Profile

We ship a strong foundation: photo, banner, headline, experience, skills, and company context if it matters.

  • Copy you can stand behind with colleagues or clients.
  • A light workflow to keep the profile current.

Content: first posts, then a system

A simple post structure, core formats (story, lesson, opinion, breakdown), early posts with support.

  • Topic brainstorm and a content plan with format rotation.
  • Posting cadence that fits your life—not “daily at any cost”.

Networking and messages

Who to reach, what to say: invite notes, first message after a connect, polite follow-ups.

  • A short priority list for the week and a daily rhythm you can actually keep.

Review and next cycle

We read the stats and the qualitative signals: what worked, what did not, where to shift weight next.

If you need a company page in sync with a personal profile, say so up front in Telegram—that is a separate block of work in the plan.

More contexts I work in

Developers who want offers

If LinkedIn is your channel for new offers and recruiter conversations, the spine is the same: a clear profile, cadence, networking, and content without forced “thought leadership.” We can stay grounded—stack, problems you solve, how you work—while respecting NDAs. Early on we align on target markets (local, remote, relocation) and tune headline, activity, and outreach accordingly.

HR brand and LinkedIn groups for executives

I also run HR brand work and LinkedIn groups where senior leaders and C-level participate: content line, cadence, engagement, alignment with employer brand and HR goals. Scope and legal steps differ from 1:1 retainer—we scope that in Telegram before kickoff.

What I need from you

  • Time for calls and for the agreed work between calls.
  • Willingness to approve copy and show up on the platform (posts, comments, DMs).
  • Clear constraints: employer rules, NDAs, industries where you cannot name cases.
